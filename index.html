Gabriela Velasco

Choreographer, Performer – Ballerina, Dance Teacher (stage dance)

Gabriela Velasco, head of sponsored international projects such as "Bicicleta em Movimento" and "Solidao à flor da Pele," has been a government-funded ballerina and choreographer from São Paulo since the age of 16. She is a co-founder of the dance company "Balé da Cidade de Taubate" (led by director Alexandra Luppe). In New York, she won the VKIBC Golden Medal. Gabriela has successfully collaborated with renowned choreographers such as Victor Navarro Capell, Luis Fernando Bongiovanni, Henrique Rodovalho, Jaruam Rodrigues, and Darion Rodrigues (Cirque de Soleil), gaining international recognition. In São Paulo and after a couple of years in a new production in Schloss Solitude, Stuttgart, she worked closely with Andrea Pivatto. She also presented at the International Dance Solo Stuttgart - Solo Choreo in Germany, with the support of Produktionszentrum Tanz & Performance e.V. Additionally, she is an experienced dance instructor. Alongside her artistic career, Gabriela has participated in various social projects in Brazil, Spain, and Cuba, teaching dance to children and seniors. Furthermore, she has taught ballet and contemporary dance at various dance schools. She has also worked in different countries such as the USA and France.

Dance Classes

Ballett Classes, Contemporary Dance, Stage Dance, Choreographic Creation


Ballet Classic

In Gabriela Velasco's ballet classic class, students immerse themselves in the timeless beauty and precision of classical ballet. Through a structured curriculum, students learn the foundational techniques of ballet, focusing on posture, alignment, and grace. Gabriela guides students through a comprehensive study of ballet vocabulary and musicality, fostering consciousness and precision in their movements. With personalized attention and constructive feedback, students develop strength, flexibility, and poise while cultivating a deep appreciation for the rich tradition of ballet.

Contemporary Dance

Gabriela Velasco's contemporary dance class offers a dynamic fusion of movement styles, drawing inspiration from modern, jazz, and ballet techniques. Influenced by pioneers like William Forsythe, students explore improvisation techniques to expand their movement vocabulary and develop a unique artistic voice. Through choreographic exploration and strategies like the dance partitures from William Forsythe, students delve into the creative process, honing their skills in dance composition and storytelling. Additionally, Gabriela infuses rhythms of Brazilian dance into the class, encouraging students to innovate movement patterns and explore their cultural identity through dance. With an emphasis on individual expression and collaboration, students are empowered to create their own choreographies and dance styles, finding their own identities through movement.
