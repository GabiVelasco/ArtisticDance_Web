Gabriela Velasco

A Fusion of Artistry and Technological Mastery

Choreographer, Performer – Ballerina, Dance Teacher (stage dance)

Gabriela Velasco, head of sponsored international projects such as "Bicicleta em Movimento" and "Solidao à flor da Pele," has been a government-funded ballerina and choreographer from São Paulo since the age of 16. She is a co-founder of the dance company "Balé da Cidade de Taubate" (led by director Alexandra Luppe). In New York, she won the VKIBC Golden Medal. Gabriela has successfully collaborated with renowned choreographers such as Victor Navarro Capell, Luis Fernando Bongiovanni, Henrique Rodovalho, Jaruam Rodrigues, and Darion Rodrigues (Cirque de Soleil), gaining international recognition. In São Paulo and after a couple of years in a new production in Schloss Solitude, Stuttgart, she worked closely with Andrea Pivatto. She also presented at the International Dance Solo Stuttgart - Solo Choreo in Germany, with the support of Produktionszentrum Tanz & Performance e.V. Additionally, she is an experienced dance instructor. Alongside her artistic career, Gabriela has participated in various social projects in Brazil, Spain, and Cuba, teaching dance to children and seniors. Furthermore, she has taught ballet and contemporary dance at various dance schools. She has also worked in different countries such as the USA and France.


Computer Science Expert - Software Development / creative IT-artist

As a Web and Software Developer, Gabriela Velasco seamlessly blends creativity with technical acumen to craft innovative digital experiences. Her proficiency extends across various programming languages and tools. Additionally, Gabriela's passion and engagement in Internet of Things (IoT) projects further demonstrate her commitment to pushing the boundaries of innovation, showcasing a rare fusion of artistry and technological mastery that transcends boundaries and inspires greatness.

REPENSTISMO - STRUKTUR IM CHAOS

This choreography introduces the audience to the improvisation of the “Repentismo“ of Brazilian popular culture, which communicates with the concept of spontaneous „Poetry Slam“ in German. The transition between dialogue (slam poetry) and rhythm (Repentismo) provides a structure, although it exists in an atmosphere of chaos. In a second moment of the piece, the artist introduces slam poetry generated by artificial intelligence. She concludes that chaos, despite its imperfections, provides natural and essential aspects in the search for the structure of one ‘s identity. How do technology and feelings communicate with each other and can be unified? All that with expressions of poetry, improvisation and structure, rhythm art and dance. With poems from artists such as Gerald Fiebig, Julia Engelman and also great poets from Brazil, are present by Gabriela Velasco with “Repentismo - Struktur im Chaos”.

ALMOST 55

About the Scenic Experiment: “Almost 55”

“Almost 55” is an ongoing dance performance by Andrea Pivatto, now also a scenic experiment with Gabriela Velasco, exploring the passage of time. Guest artists participate worldwide, emphasizing audience interaction to manipulate time and engage in scenic games reflecting diverse existential concerns.

About us

Renowned choreographers Andrea Pivatto and Gabriela Velasco reunite once again to present “Almost 55” – a captivating scenic experiment exploring the passage of time. Their journey began in São Paulo, where they worked closely within the vibrant dance community, gaining international recognition for their collaborations. In each city, Andrea invites a guest artist to be part of this immersive experience. The performance invites audience interaction, with opportunities to manipulate time and engage in scenic games, reflecting diverse existential concerns.
Duration: 90 minutes

"Solitude on the Surface"

- A Solidão à Flor da Pele

An essay on loneliness - In Memory of Ahyrton Ferreira Campos

In 2012, I was fortunate to receive support from the Ministry of Culture (MINC) for the presentation of my essay "Solitude on the Surface" at the Havana Vieja Ciudad en Movimiento Festival in Cuba. At the festival, I conducted workshops on contemporary dance infused with elements of Brazilian folk dance for audiences of all ages, including children. Additionally, I showcased my choreography, "Solitude on the Surface," a piece crafted by myself, Gabriela Velasco. This choreography was not only a creative expression but also a research of movements and essence inspired by butoh dance. It provided a unique opportunity to explore the depths of emotion, allowing us to dance with the memories of loved ones who are no longer with us, including my grandfather, Ahyrton.

Dance Classes

Ballett Classes, Contemporary Dance, Stage Dance, Choreographic Creation


Ballet Classic

In Gabriela Velasco's ballet classes, students embark on a journey into the timeless beauty and precision of classical ballet. Classical ballet is the foundation of every form of dance, and under Gabriela's expert guidance, students dive into the fascinating world of classical ballet. Through a structured curriculum, students learn fundamental techniques focusing on posture, alignment, and grace. Gabriela guides students through a comprehensive study of ballet vocabulary and musicality, fostering awareness and precision in their movements. With personalized attention and constructive feedback, students develop strength, flexibility, and poise while cultivating a deep appreciation for the rich tradition of ballet.

Contemporary Dance

In Gabriela Velasco's contemporary dance class, students explore diverse movement languages like Contact Improvisation, Forsythe Technique, Ballet, Modern, and Brazilian dance, fostering freedom and creativity. Inspired by daily revolutions in contemporary dance, students expand their movement vocabulary and develop unique artistic voices, guided by Gabriela's expertise. Through choreographic exploration and the infusion of Brazilian rhythms, students innovate and express their cultural identities, empowered to create choreographies and styles, leading to artistic growth and self-discovery.
